Biography

James Mwangi joined Africa Bora Soccer Academy in 2021 and has been instrumental in developing our youngest players. His passion for working with children and deep understanding of youth development has made him a favorite among players and parents alike.

Before joining the academy, James coached at local primary schools and ran community soccer clinics. His ability to make training fun while instilling fundamental skills has helped dozens of young players develop a lasting love for the game.

Coaching Experience

  • 2021-Present: U12 Head Coach, Africa Bora Soccer Academy
  • 2018-2021: Youth Coach, Nairobi Community Sports Center
  • 2016-2018: Assistant Coach, St. Mary's Primary School
  • 2015-2016: Volunteer Coach, Youth Outreach Program

Playing Background

James played youth soccer through secondary school, where he developed his technical skills and understanding of the game. While a knee injury ended his playing career early, it sparked his interest in coaching and helping the next generation of players achieve their dreams.

Certifications & Education

  • CAF C Coaching License (2019)
  • Youth Development Certificate (2020)
  • First Aid & CPR Certified (2025)
  • Diploma in Physical Education, Kenyatta University (2015)

Coaching Philosophy

"At this age, the most important thing is to help kids fall in love with soccer. If they're having fun and learning the basics properly, everything else will follow. I want every player to leave training with a smile on their face and excitement for the next session."

James emphasizes positive reinforcement and individual progress over winning at all costs. He believes that by building confidence and fundamental skills in a supportive environment, young players develop the foundation they need for future success.

Achievements

  • Led U12 team to Regional Youth League Runner-Up finish (2025)
  • Developed three players who advanced to U15 team ahead of schedule
  • Recognized as "Youth Coach of the Year" by local sports association (2024)
